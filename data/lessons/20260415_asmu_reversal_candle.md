# ASMU — Reversal Candle After Parabolic Run

**Date:** 2026-04-15
**Symbol:** ASMU
**Action:** BUY → Manual Close
**Entry:** $24.92 | **Exit:** ~$24.37 | **P&L:** -$0.55 (-2.2%)

## What Happened

ASMU ran from ~$22 to $27.11 over several days with no fundamental news catalyst. On Apr 15, the stock gapped down hard — opened at $25.54, hit a low of $24.25, and closed at $24.37. That's a 10% intraday drop (1.5x ATR), printing a textbook distribution / reversal candle (open near high, close near low).

## Technicals at Exit

- RSI dropped from 60.5 → 52.4 (momentum fading fast)
- Price rejected at the upper Bollinger Band ($27.20)
- MACD histogram shrinking despite still being positive
- No news to explain the move — pure profit-taking / exhaustion

## Lesson

- When a stock runs 20%+ in a few days with no fundamental catalyst, **tighten stops aggressively**.
- A gap-down open after a parabolic move is a strong sell signal.
- Do not hold through reversal candles on low-float momentum names — the move down can be just as violent as the move up.
- Consider taking partial profits after extended runs rather than waiting for a stop to trigger.
